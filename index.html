Walter E. Diemer

The inventor of bubblegum

  • He was born January 8, 1904
  • He was an accountant at Fleer in 1926
  • He made bubblegum pink because it was the only dye in the factory
  • He used a saltwater taffy machine to wrap the bubble gum
  • His first test market was at a local mom-and-pop candy store
  • His first piece of gum was 1 cent and sold out in a day
  • He taught a salesman to blow bubbles as a selling point for the gum
  • He did not patent his invention, and it became a popular treat during the Great Depression
  • He stayed with Fleer for decades and became senior vice president as well as a member of the Board of Directors
  • He died on his birthday, January 8, in 1998
